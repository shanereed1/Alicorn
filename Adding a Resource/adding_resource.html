Adding a Resource

Required*

Links should be direct links to available online resources.

Use the existing title of the resource you linked or write your own. The title you use will be visible and will act as a direct link to the resource.
Choose the topic that best fits the main theme of the resource.

If you find none of the available topics are a good fit for your resource, place it in the “User Experience” topic. If you save a resource with the wrong topic, you can change it from your profile page.
Tags are for your own personal use to organize resources you post. Write them however you feel comfortable because you’re the only one who can see them.

There’s no limit to how many tags you add to a resource. When writing tags make sure to separate tags with a comma. If you need to view or edit your tags at anytime you can do this by referring to your profile or the resource itself.

Writing Example: (Sketching, UX, data analytics, user_research, author's.name)
Use this field to summarize or describe the resource. Talk about how the resource helped you learn something new or answered a question.Write as much or little as you need to communicate your thoughts clearly.

To revise after posting a resource, visit your profile, find the resource, and click on “edit”.

For guidance, you’ll find examples of good summaries here.
Notifications tell you when another user upvotes or comments on a resource you’ve posted.

View notifications in the notifications tab on the homepage. Notification settings for each post can be edited on the resource’s page; once there, find and click “edit” and uncheck “notifications”.
